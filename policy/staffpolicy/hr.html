Advocacy Policy and Procedure

Introduction

Decent Care strives to be recognised as an Employer of Choice in ensuring that team members are supported within their roles to provide quality services to participants. The purpose of this policy and procedure is to set out Decent Care’s Human Resources arrangements.

Scope

This policy applies to the Managing Directors, Program Managers, team members and contractors who may undertake work at Decent Care.

Principles

Decent Care provides an equitable, safe, and encouraging workplace. All team members are valued and recognised for their contribution to Decent Care’s successful outcomes. Flexible and supportive working conditions are provided in line with relevant legislation and comparable to industry standards.

Rights of Decent Care team members
Decent Care team members have the right to:

  • Be treated with courtesy, respect, integrity and kindness while they work.
  • Feel safe from physical abuse, verbal harassment and violence.
  • Be supported to work in the way they have been trained and work within their scope of practice
  • Discuss, with the Managing Directors, an alternative, if they feel the task that they are being asked to undertake is unsafe or there is an identified risk.
  • Have their personal information and circumstances confidential at all times.

Any feedback or complaints can be lodged by filling out the ‘Complaints Management and Feedback’ form or if situations need immediate attention contact should be made directly with the Managing Director of Operations.

Policy

Decent Care team members must meet the qualification and experience requirements and be competent in providing person-centred support. All team members must complete the mandatory NDIS orientation module. Additionally, all team members must have, and maintain, a clear NDIS Worker Screening Check and Working with Children Check in order to provide supports to NDIS participants. Records of the criminal history screening status, qualifications, experience, training and the professional development of all team members are held in their relevant HR files. Decent Care prides itself on being an equal opportunity employer and takes all necessary measures to protect its team members from bullying and harassment.

Procedure

Legal Responsibilities
Decent Care develops and implements human resource management systems that comply with relevant employment legislation, including:

  • Long Service Leave Act 2018
  • Occupational Health and Safety Act 2004 (NSW)
  • Workplace Injury Management and Workers Compensation Act 1998
  • (Commonwealth) Superannuation Guarantee (Administration) Act 1992.

Decent Care requires that the entire team contributes to a workplace free from discrimination and that they comply with anti-discrimination legislation, including:

  • Disability Discrimination Act 1992 (Commonwealth)
  • Human Rights and Equal Opportunity Commission Act 1996 (Commonwealth)
  • Racial Discrimination Act 1975 (Commonwealth)
  • Sex Discrimination Act 1984 (Commonwealth).

Industrial award
Decent Care team members will be employed under the Social, Community, Home Care and Disability Services Industry Award 2010 (SCHADS 2010).

Shifts
The minimum shift booked for is 2 hours. Team members may also be requested to fill in for a late vacancy.

Support Provision
Decent Care provides support to people with disability who are participants of the National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS)
When delivering supports to NDIS participants, ideally team members will have the qualification and experience requirements of a minimum Certificate III in Disability Support or similar.
In addition to the above requirements, all team members must have, where relevant:

  • 100 points of Identification
  • Working with Children Check
  • NDIS Worker Screening Check
  • International Criminal History Check (where relevant)
  • Right to work in Australia
  • Professional Registrations (where appropriate)
  • Job Descriptions
  • Licence and appropriate insurance where travel and transport are being provided
  • NDIS Worker Orientation Module Completion Certificate
  • Vaccination Records

Contact Details (including Secondary Employment contact details)
In the case of a natural disaster or emergency, Decent Care may require additional team members to assist participants to action their Emergency Plan. Team members must notify Decent Care of any changes within 48 hours of the change. Team members details must be obtained on recruitment and reviewed for currency at Annual Performance Reviews.
This includes:

  • Name, phone number, email address and residential address
  • Team members secondary contact e.g family
  • Secondary employment contact details

The selection and recruitment of team members is based on merit, with the best possible candidate identified through their curriculum vitae, responses to the position selection criteria, interview process and reference checks.
All Decent Care team members have a position description that outlines:

  • Primary functions
  • Accountabilities
  • Lines of reporting
  • Essential and desirable skills, knowledge and experience.

All position advertisements will identify:

  • Position title
  • Summary of the primary functions
  • Process for applying
  • Contact details for enquiries
  • Application closing dates.

Applicants that meet the essential skills, knowledge and experience are offered an interview. Applicants that meet some of the desirable skills, knowledge and experience may be offered an interview dependent on the number and quality of other applicants. Interviewees are rated against the essential skills, knowledge, and experience as well as interview questions relevant to the position. All unsuccessful interviewees are advised by a phone call, followed by a formal written notification. Unless otherwise determined in the interview, remuneration package is negotiated at offer of the position. All relevant screening checks will be carried out prior to commencement of employment. All position enquiries, applications and interviews are treated confidentially and access to such details is restricted to those directly involved in the recruitment and selection process.

NDIS Worker Screening Checks
Each person who works for Decent Care, will be assessed whether they are in a risk related role. For each person who is deemed as a “risk assessed role”, it is mandatory that they undertake and NDIS Worker Screening Check at their own cost. Further information about how to apply should be sourced from the team members Program Manager.

International Police Checks
If a prospective team member has resided continuously in an overseas country for 12 months or more in the last ten years, they must contact the relevant overseas police force to obtain a criminal or police record check. If they were a minor when they were overseas, they do not require an international police check. Some countries will not release such information and in these extenuating cases, a statutory declaration and character reference checks must be provided. The reference checks must be received from at least two individuals who personally knew the person overseas. The applicant must be informed that referees will be asked whether they have knowledge or information concerning the applicant, which would adversely affect the applicant from performing the job, including any relevant criminal offences. The credentials of persons acting as referees must be verified and can include previous employers, government officials and family members.In the case of asylum seekers and refugees who may be unable to provide character references to accompany a statutory declaration, the statutory declaration will suffice with proof of status.

Right to Work in Australia
Decent Care will confirm team members right to work in Australia if they have recently come to Australia from overseas. Check the Department of Immigration & Border Protection’s Visa Entitlement Verification Online (VEVO) checking system at http://www.border.gov.au/VEVO.

Identity, qualifications, and referees
Decent Care will confirm the identity (through 100-point photo identification), qualifications (through sighting a copy) and referees of all prospective team members prior to their appointment.
If there are doubts about a person’s qualification, the Managing Directors will undertake an online check to verify that the qualification was awarded to the applicant. If an online check is not possible, the person should provide a letter from the registrar of the relevant educational institution confirming the qualification’s validity. If there are any concerns about the authenticity of the qualification as presented, the issuing institution must be contacted directly to verify that the particular qualification was completed and issued to the person on the date specified.

Vaccination Records
As a Disability Support Organisation, Decent Care works with participants who are vulnerable to illness and low immunity. It is a mandatory requirement under the State Government that team members working within the Disability Sector must be fully vaccinated. Records of all vaccinations (and mandated boosters) are recorded within team members files.

NDIS Code of Conduct
All team members will be provided with a copy of the NDIS Code of Conduct upon engagement. This is located within the organisational code of conduct. Training regarding the code of conduct is included within the induction process. Anyone can raise a complaint about potential breaches of the NDIS Code of Conduct. Should a participant or other stakeholder wish to make a complaint about Decent Care in respect to the NDIS Code of Conduct, they will be directed to the Complaints and Feedback Policy. Should Decent Care, or people employed or otherwise engaged by Decent Care be found to have breached the NDIS Code of Conduct, Decent Care will comply with any education, compliance or enforcement action stipulated by the NDIS Commissioner.

Induction, Training and Development
Decent Care’s Business Continuity Plan identifies how Decent Care will source and induct a workforce in the event that workforce disruptions occur in an emergency or disaster.

Induction
All new team members are required to participate in an induction and orientation process, led by their Program Manager, for the purpose of familiarising the new team members with Decent Care’s operations. This includes an overview of Decent Care policies, code of conduct and expectations of team members and the requirements of the position. The Code of Conduct Agreement is signed by team members to acknowledge the completion of the induction and orientation process. The signed original is to be held on the personnel file and a copy is provided to the new team members.
At commencement of employment, the team members receives an orientation pack that includes:

  • Copies of the position description, contract of employment and code of conduct
  • Access to the Policy and Procedure documents
  • Access to all internal forms through a QR Code system

All permanent or contract-based team members are under a three (3) month probationary period at commencement of employment. This period allows for Decent Care and the new team members to determine if the person is suitable for the position before continuation of the employment contract.


Training and Development
Decent Care will identify, plan, facilitate, record, and evaluate the effectiveness of training and development for team members, to ensure they are capable in their roles and meet the needs of each participant. The following mandatory training will be provided to all relevant team members each year. Each team member’s training obligations under the NDIS Practice Standards and other NDIS Rules include:

  • Prevention of Violence, Abuse, Neglect, Exploitation and Discrimination
  • Emergency and Disaster Management
  • Complaints Management
  • Incident Management
  • Work Health and Safety
  • Positive Behavioural Support and Restrictive Practice Implementation
  • Mealtime Management
  • Infection Control (including Covid19)
  • Use and disposal of Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

Working Conditions
Wages
All team members wages will be paid into their chosen bank accounts fortnightly. Superannuation contributions will be paid directly into their chosen fund in accordance with the Superannuation Guarantee (Administration) Act 1992 (Commonwealth). Any travel in a team members personal vehicle on shift and laundry allowances will be included in their wages and can be identified on their pay slip.

Transporting Participants and Travel
The vehicle used to transport the participant must be registered and roadworthy with compulsory third-party insurance. All kilometres travelled in a team member’s vehicle whilst on shift are claimable on their time sheet, claimable travel includes travel with a participant e.g., a social engagement or appointment or from one participant to another. Team members are unable to claim travel from their place of residence to the allocated shift and return or if team members are required to use the participant’s vehicle. Travelling between participants appointments will be aimed to be kept at a minimum of time/kilometres.

Working from Home:
Where duties are undertaken by team members in their homes rather than in the office, Work, Health and Safety considerations apply. A working from home assessment is to be completed and actioned before working from home commences.

Insurance
Decent Care team members are covered under Workers Compensation Insurance and are covered by Decent Care’s Professional Indemnity and Public Liability Insurance. Contracted team members are required to maintain their own Professional Indemnity and Public Liability Insurance. As a part of Decent Care’s policies and procedures, team members are required to disclose in writing any prior or chronic injuries that they have sustained or affect their ability to work.

Casual team members unavailability
If a team member is sick or unable to fulfill their duties for the arranged shifts, they are required to notify their Program Manager with a minimum of 2 hours or at the earliest convenience so other arrangements can be made for the participant. Casual team members are not eligible for sick leave. If team members are required to make themselves unavailable for a period of time, they are required to notify their Program Manager via email at least one week in advance.

Leave entitlement
Full-time team members are entitled to a range of leave entitlements as outlined below; part-time team members will be entitled to the below leave entitlements on a pro-rata basis.

  • Recreational Leave: A period of four (4) weeks or twenty (20) working days paid recreational leave will apply for each twelve (12) month period for full time team members. Recreational leave is exclusive of any days declared and observed as Public Holidays as notified by the State and/or Australian Government. Recreational leave may be taken after the completion of six (6) months service, unless negotiated with the Managing Directors. Payment for untaken Recreational Leave at the termination of employment is exclusive of leave loading.
  • Personal and Carers Leave: A period of ten (10) days per year paid personal leave may be taken for personal illness or injury (Sick Leave), additionally leave may be taken if a child or dependent is sick and requires the team member’s care and support (Carer’s Leave). Two (2) days are available for bereavement on the death of an immediate family or household member (Bereavement Leave). Accrued Sick Leave will not be paid out on termination of employment.
  • Parental Leave: Team members are able to take parental leave if they have worked for their employer for at least 12 months. For casual team members to be eligible for unpaid parental leave they need to have worked for their employer on a regular and systematic basis for at least 12 month with a reasonable expectation of continuing work with the employer on a regular and systematic basis, had it not been for the birth or adoption of a child.
  • Long Service Leave: Long Service Leave entitlements are provided in accordance with the Long Service Leave Act with the exception that team members are entitled to access Long Service Leave after 7 years continuous service at a pro rata rate. No leave loading applies to Long Service Leave. Requests for leave are made through the Application for Leave Form and approved by the relevant supervisor in advance to the leave being taken.
  • Unpaid Pandemic Leave: any team member is entitled to take up to 2 weeks’ unpaid leave if they are required by government or medical authorities or on the advice of a medical practitioner to self-isolate and are consequently prevented from working or are otherwise prevented from working by measures taken by government or medical authorities in response to the COVID-19 pandemic. Team members must notify their Team Leader of taking the leave and why they require the leave as soon as practicable.

Standards of Work
Team Members Responsibilities
Under the WHS Act 2011 and OHS Act 2004, team members are responsible for identifying and reporting any hazards, issues, incidents or near misses that occur in the workplace as soon as possible. This is to be reported on a Decent Care’s WHS Incident Hazard Report Form. Team members Program Managers will follow up on any incident or hazard reported by team members in a timely manner.
Decent Care team members:

  • Must take reasonable care of their own health and safety and the health and safety of those in the participant’s home, including family members and other Decent Care team members
  • Ensure that during their work, their behaviour does not put anyone else at risk.
  • Are to only work within the scope of practice they have been trained in.
  • Are to comply with Decent Care policies, procedures and the NDIS Code of Conduct.

Bullying, Harassment and Violence
Where allegations of discrimination, bullying and harassment are made, Decent Care employs a zero-tolerance policy. Any team members that is found to have engaged in such conduct will be warned or disciplined. Severe or repeat occurrences may lead to formal discipline up to and including dismissal. Where a team member believes they have been discriminated against, bullied, harassed, or victimised they should report the incident to the Managing Directors or their Program Manager using the Complaints and Feedback Policy. Team members who wish to remain anonymous can submit their report in writing to The Managing Directors at: 41 Hartnett Dr, Seaford VIC 3198.

Introduction Session with participants
All participants will be introduced to team members through a meet and greet prior to support delivery. This enables team members to be identifiable and familiar to participants as Decent Care team members.

Alcohol and Drugs
Decent Care has a zero-tolerance policy for the use of alcohol and illegal drugs during work time or the effects of alcohol or drugs. If a team member is found to be affected by drugs or alcohol whilst working for Decent Care, they will be asked to leave work immediately until further investigations is undertaken.

Smoking
Decent Care team members are not to smoke or vape in any participant’s homes and are to refrain from smoking or vaping whilst out in public with the participants.

Grievances
Where team members wish to lodge a formal complaint or grievance, they must complete a complaints and feedback form and submit this to their Program Manager or the Managing Director of Operations. Team members who wish to remain anonymous can submit their report in writing 41 Hartnett Dr, Seaford VIC 3198. Once a formal grievance is lodged, the Managing Director of Operations or a delegate representative will investigate the matter in line with Decent Care Complaints and Feedback Policy. If the Managing Director of Operations has a conflict of interest in the matter, the Managing Director of service delivery will conduct the investigation.

Whistle-Blower
Decent Care will support and respect any individual who acts as a whistle-blower to draw attention to suspected inappropriate, corrupt, or illegal conduct or behaviour. Any person can report conduct that is believed to be unlawful or immoral to the Managing Directors verbally, or in writing without fear of retribution. Where a person makes a claim, it must be documented and dated and must be signed by the whistle-blower to verify the contents as a true account. During the investigation, the Managing Directors will keep the whistle-blower and all other parties informed about the process and the outcome of the investigation where permitted. Investigation of an allegation will be confidential, fair, and objective. Any information shared about the allegations raised by a whistle-blower will be de-identified to protect the whistle-blower’s identity and stored securely while the matter is examined. If the matter is investigated by an external organisation or referred to police or another investigating body, there may be limits on what information can be shared. In this case, involved parties will be advised of any limitations on the release of information.

Record Keeping
Decent Care will maintain records for all team members, including their identity, qualifications, professional registrations and criminal history check status. Supporting documents must be sighted, copied and kept on each team member’s record. Records of induction, training and organisational and professional development provided to all team members must also be kept on individual team members’ records and in Decent Care’s Training and Development Register. The Program Managers are responsible for overseeing team members’ training and development needs and will track training undertaken and future needs in Decent Care Training and Development Register. All training will be tested for effectiveness post-delivery.
Personnel Records
Decent Care maintains individual personnel files for current team members where information on recruitment, selection, position description, employment contracts, financials, performance, and development are kept. Records are kept on the DC Cloud which is password protected with authorised access.

Performance Management

Supervision
Supervision will be undertaken by the Service Manager. This can be undertaken by phone, and relevant notes taken. Supervision additionally also occurs in team meetings as an agenda item where group discussions can occur around experiences or policy settings. Supervision notes are kept on team members files.

Annual Performance Appraisal
Team members appraisals are undertaken by Program Managers and where required the Managing Director of Operations on an annual 12-month basis. The aim of the appraisal is to gauge both Decent Care’s and team members performance and to identify any training needs required to develop the skills and organisation scope of service. If the team member is a family member or relation, performance appraisal will be undertaken by an appropriate senior team member. The Performance Appraisal process must include reviewing the team members contact information for currency.

Disciplinary Action
Team members will be given sufficient written notice of the intention to meet with them to discuss the conduct issues. They will be provided with an opportunity to bring a support person should they wish to do so. A meeting will be held with the team member to hear their response to the misconduct. This is an opportunity for them to explain their side of the story and provide an explanation for their behaviour. At this meeting the Program Manager/Managing Director will not provide an outcome as they require time to reasonably consider the response. In certain circumstances, the Program Manager/Managing Director may be required to undertake further steps before providing an outcome to the team members. This may include interviewing witnesses or obtaining further evidence. After allowing sufficient time for Program Manager/Managing Director to consider the team member’s response and having reviewed any further materials (witness statements or written evidence) an outcome meeting should be scheduled.
The Program Manager/Managing Director will provide team members with a fair outcome, this may include no further action, verbal or written warning or termination of employment. Serious misconduct by team members that may lead to summary dismissal includes such things as: proven theft, physical assault, gross negligence, serious breach of policies and/or procedures, wilful or malicious damage, serious breach of safety procedures, illegal or fraudulent acts while representing Decent Care, and/or abandonment of employment. Serious misconduct by team members will be investigated and documented by the Managing Directors as part of the dismissal process

Ceasing Employment
Cessation of employment by either Decent Care or the team members shall reflect the stipulated requirements of the designated Award (or determined by Fair Work Australia), except for those in relation to redundancy. If redundancy action is initiated by the Managing Directors, they will be required to give four (4) weeks-notice to any team members who is made redundant and the team members shall be entitled to payments equivalent to all untaken Recreational Leave and Long Service Leave. Except in the case of serious misconduct, as determined by the Managing Directors, payment in lieu of the notice shall be paid if the appropriate notice is not given. Decent Care must comply with all State and Federal legislation and the team member’s Employment Contract in relation to performance management and employment termination. Decent Care must ensure:

  • Dismissal is not for an unfair reason
  • The team members knows the reason for dismissal and has an opportunity to respond in relation to that reason; and
  • They provide the team members with appropriate notice or compensation in lieu of notice.

Team members may be dismissed on the basis of:

  • Their conduct, capacity or performance, including breaching the NDIS Code of Conduct
  • Operational requirements, e.g. the position is no longer required
  • Other reasons sufficient to justify termination

Team members Resignation
Team members are required to provide Decent Care with relevant notice as per their employment contract in the event they choose to end their employment with the business. This notice must be provided in writing. Decent Care has the discretion to pay the team members their notice period in lieu of having them attend work for the notice period. Decent Care will ensure all salary and entitlements are paid to the team members within 14 days of the end of their employment with Decent Care.

  • Addressing performance issues with team members

The Service Manager is responsible for:

  • Maintaining a Screening Register that contains the Clearance Numbers, Expiry Dates and Currency Status of all checks for all team members
  • Following up team members who have expired checks and ensure no shifts are allocated until the check has been undertaken
  • Undertaking Supervision

Program Managers are responsible for:

  • Undertaking performance appraisals
  • Providing information and support to team members to obtain their check
  • Identifying training needs and notifying the Managing Directors

Team members are responsible for:

  • Providing required documentation for their HR File
  • Sourcing their screening checks and adding Decent Care as their employer
  • Advising the Managing Directors if they are charged with a criminal offence which is punishable by imprisonment or, if found guilty, could affect their ability to meet the requirements of their job.

Sexual Harassment Any form of unwanted, unwelcome or uninvited sexual behaviour that is offensive, humiliating or embarrassing
Whistle-Blower a person who informs on a person or organization regarded as engaging in an unlawful or immoral activity.
